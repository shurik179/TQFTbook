    About

    Lectures on TQFT is a set of online lecture notes on topological quantum field theory and, more generally, extended TQFT. They are based on a course taught by Alexander Kirillov at Stony Brook University in Spring 2026. This is a work in progress and is updated frequently.

    A few quick facts:

    1. The notes aim to be a readable overview rather than a complete treatment: in many places only a sketch of a definition or proof is given, with references to the original papers for full details.

    2. They survey TQFT from its origins in physics and Atiyah's axioms, through extended TQFT following the work of Lurie, and possibly to recent developments related to factorization homology.

    3. The emphasis is on examples, including Dijkgraaf–Witten (finite gauge) theory, 2d TQFT via symmetric Frobenius algebras (both through generators and relations and through state sums), Turaev–Viro 3d theory, and Crane–Yetter 4d / Chern–Simons 3d theory.

    4. They are meant to be read online: with hyperlinks and the search function you can quickly browse to the definitions, lemmas, and theorems that a given result depends on.

    5. We use tags as permanent identifiers for results, so that external references stay valid even as the text is reorganized. You can read more on the tags explained page.
